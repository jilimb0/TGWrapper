# Community Operating Model & Hub

This document defines how we manage and grow the TGWrapper community, gather user feedback, and organize community contributions.

---

## 🧭 The Community Hubs

- **[GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/jilimb0/tgwrapper/discussions)** — Our official community space for:
  - **Q&A:** Technical questions and troubleshooting.
  - **Show & Tell / Success Stories:** Showcasing what you've built.
  - **Ideas / RFCs:** Requesting core architectural changes.
- **[GitHub Issues](https://github.com/jilimb0/tgwrapper/issues)** — Strictly reserved for reproducible bug reports and structured feature request submissions.
- **[Telegram Community Chat](https://t.me/tgwrapper_community)** *(placeholder)* — Instant chat for real-time developer networking.

---

## 🔁 Community Cadence & Interactions

Maintainers follow a standard routine to keep the community active and supported:

### 1. Weekly Q&A Triage
Every Friday, maintainers review unanswered Q&A threads in Discussions. If a thread describes a reproducible core issue, it is converted to a GitHub Issue.

### 2. Monthly RFC Summary
On the first of every month, maintainers publish a "Next Month Roadmap" discussion thread summarizing proposed RFC designs and seeking community feedback.

### 3. Contributor Recognition Spotlights
Every second week, the project highlights standout community-submitted PRs, adapters, or tutorials on our social channels and repository updates.

---

## 📥 Feedback Loops

We systematically categorize user feedback into structured paths:

- **Docs Friction:** Fixes are committed directly within the week.
- **Migration Blockers:** Assigned to the next minor version scope or documented as comparison recipes in [CODE_COMPARISONS.md](../CODE_COMPARISONS.md).
- **Missing Integrations:** Tracked as upcoming target candidate plugins in [ECOSYSTEM.md](../ECOSYSTEM.md) to encourage community contribution.
